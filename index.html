11 Mixtape

⚠️ Warning: This project is being developed by a teenager with basic experience. The kernel is experimental. Expect bugs, errors, and confusion. Use at your own risk. This is not maintained by a professional team or adult experts.

11 Mixtape is an experimental kernel project designed to explore the foundations of secure and minimal operating system design. Unlike traditional kernels, it focuses on fortification, deliberate isolation, and explicit tradeoffs rather than broad compatibility or convenience. Users can experiment with its early design and learn from its architecture, but the system is highly experimental, may contain bugs, and should not be used as a primary operating system.

The kernel aims to provide a platform for understanding how minimal, security-focused systems behave. It includes a basic shell and package manager as a starting point, and future versions may expand toward more complex features like sound support or desktop environments. Every aspect of development is openly documented, but the project is maintained solely by a single developer.
